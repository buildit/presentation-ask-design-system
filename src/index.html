Ask not what your design system can do for you...

A presentation about design systems

[Presenters introduce themselves]

Design systems are a hot topic today. They seem to be the magic solution to all our woes.

But, where did they come from?

What problems do they actually solve?

And, crucially, what do they not solve?

What, the actual fuck, is a design system anyway?

Raise your hand if you've heard of "design systems".

Keep your hand up if you can explain to a friend what they are.

Keep your hand up if you have worked on a design system.

Don't worry - all shall be revealed...

Let's take a step back and look at how we've ended up with design systems...

Does this scenario ring any bells?

You're the developer on a project and the first time you get see actual visual designs, they've already been reviewed and signed off by the client.

For the 5 page microsite you're making, you count 4 distinct button designs, 3 modal overlay styles and 7 different font sizes.

The layout for the "desktop" design demands a different ordering of HTML elements than the corresponding "mobile" design.

All the placeholder text for headings, user names and product features fits perfectly on one line.

Oh! And, it must work on IE8.

Or how about this one?

The 250 page "brand guidelines" PDF that the marketing department sent you, specifies a special typeface for all headings.

You ask for a web font version of it.

You are sent some TTF files.

You explain that you need the web font versions of it.

Several emails later you are sent the same TTF files.

None of the TTF files you got are for the weight specified by the brand guidelines for heading text.

Drink a shot if this seems familiar:

You've produced some visual designs and need to share them with the team.

Access to InVision, ZeroHeight and Zeplin is blocked by the company's network.

Access to Dropbox, OneDrive and WeTransfer is also blocked.

You attach your designs to an email and hit send. It bounces back - "maximum attachment size exceeded"

You try to use a USB stick but discover that IT has diabled removable media on your machine.

Someone tells you that there's an MS SharePoint site on the intranet you can use.

You lose the will to live.

...more examples for designer, stakeholder and PM pains
Evolution: Static, full-page design + build --> Modular builds + styleguide docs --> Template languages (PHP -> Mustache -> React...) --> Styleguide websites (aka pattern libraries) --> UI libraries Also: Key milestones: Separation of concerns, mobile first, progressive enhancement... At each step of the way: 1) Sub-optimal way of doing things 2) Better way is established 3) Better way introduces new questions & challenges 4) GOTO: 1)
And so, my fellow webmasters: ask not what your design system can do for you
— ask what you can do for your design system.
